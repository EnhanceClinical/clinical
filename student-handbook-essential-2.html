Welcome to the Enhance Physio Essential Program. This handbook has been designed to ensure you are fully prepared for your placement — practically, professionally, and personally.

We take our responsibility as a clinical educator seriously. This means providing you with rich learning experiences, structured supervision, and honest feedback. In return, we ask that you uphold the professional and ethical standards that our patients, staff, and community expect.

Parking

Parking availability varies by site. Contact your Supervisor before your first day to confirm the best parking option for your location. Do not use designated patient parking bays.

Travel Between Sites

On occasion, students may be required to travel between sites (e.g. Albury, Lavington, and Wodonga operate in a cluster). If inter-site travel is required during your placement:

  • Your Supervisor will inform you in advance
  • Travel is not reimbursed unless agreed in writing prior to commencement
  • You are responsible for your own transport and must hold a valid licence if driving
  • You must never transport patients in your personal vehicle
  • Travel time does not count as supervised clinical hours unless explicitly confirmed by your Supervisor

Hydrotherapy

Hydrotherapy is available at select sites (e.g. Albury). If your placement involves hydrotherapy, additional requirements apply:

  • You must disclose any relevant skin conditions, wounds, or contraindications in your health disclosure
  • Appropriate swimwear must be worn at all times — no exceptions
  • You must not enter the pool unless directed by your Supervisor
  • Hydrotherapy patient groups require additional supervision — you are an assistant, not a lead clinician
  • Patient privacy and dignity must be maintained throughout all pool interactions

Group Exercise Programs

All sites facilitate group exercise programs. As a student, your role in groups is to assist under direct supervision. You may be asked to lead warm-up activities or guide patients through exercises only once explicitly authorised by your Supervisor.

Student Expectations
What we expect from you — and what you can expect from us

Attendance & Punctuality

  • Arrive on time — or early. Punctuality is a professional expectation, not a preference.
  • If you are unwell or unable to attend, you must contact your Supervisor before your shift begins — not after.
  • Absences must be reported to both your Supervisor and your university placement coordinator.
  • Chronic lateness or unexplained absences may result in your placement being terminated.

Dress Code & Presentation

  • Neat, professional clinical attire at all times. No torn, overly casual, or revealing clothing.
  • Closed-toe shoes with non-slip soles (mandatory for patient safety).
  • Hair tied back when providing or assisting with patient care.
  • Nails kept short and clean. No strong perfume or cologne.
  • Name badge or identification to be worn visibly at all times.
  • Enhance Physio branded clothing, if provided, must be worn appropriately.

Mobile Phone Use

  • Personal phones must be on silent during all clinical hours.
  • No personal calls during patient contact time.
  • Photography and video recording on clinic premises is strictly prohibited without written authorisation. This includes recording patients, staff, or any clinical environment.
  • Phones may be used during supervised breaks in non-clinical areas only.

Your Dual Obligation

You are bound by two sets of requirements simultaneously: those of your educational institution and those of Enhance Physiotherapy. Where any conflict arises, you must notify both parties immediately. One set of requirements does not override the other — you must satisfy both.

Expectations of Enhance Physio to You

In return, Enhance Physio commits to:

  • Providing structured supervision from a qualified clinician
  • Giving honest, timely, and constructive feedback
  • Offering exposure to a diverse range of clinical presentations
  • Treating you with dignity and professionalism
  • Maintaining a safe working environment
  • Communicating any concerns about your performance promptly and fairly

Student Wellbeing: Enhance Physiotherapy is not your treating healthcare provider. Students are not entitled to complimentary or discounted clinical treatment from Enhance Physio staff. If you sustain an injury or require treatment, you must seek care through your own healthcare provider. See the Health Disclosure section for more information.

Supervision & Scope of Practice
Understanding your boundaries as a student clinician

Core Rule: You must never provide patient care, treatment, or clinical advice without the explicit supervision or delegation of your qualified Supervisor. This is non-negotiable and applies at all times, including when your Supervisor is briefly out of the room.

What Supervision Means

Supervision at Enhance Physio operates on a spectrum. Your Supervisor will clarify your level at each stage:

LevelDescriptionStudent Action
DirectSupervisor is physically present and watchingProceed only with verbal approval given in the moment
ProximateSupervisor is nearby and immediately availableMay proceed with delegated tasks; call for help if uncertain
IndirectSupervisor available but not physically presentOnly for clearly delegated, low-risk tasks — never assessment or treatment decisions

What You May Do

  • Observe clinical assessments and treatments
  • Assist with tasks explicitly delegated by your Supervisor
  • Guide group exercise participants through prescribed exercises (when authorised)
  • Assist with patient transfers and mobility (when trained and supervised)
  • Prepare equipment and treatment spaces
  • Document under Supervisor review and co-signature

What You Must NOT Do

  • Provide assessment findings or clinical diagnoses to patients
  • Give clinical advice or treatment recommendations without Supervisor approval
  • Modify a treatment plan without discussing with your Supervisor
  • Perform any manual therapy techniques without direct supervision and explicit authorisation
  • Discuss other patients' clinical details with the patient you are treating
  • Answer billing, insurance, or medico-legal questions from patients

When You Are Uncertain

If in doubt — stop and ask. Asking for clarification is a sign of professional maturity, not weakness. There is no clinical situation where it is safer to guess than to ask. Your Supervisor expects you to seek guidance when uncertain. Failure to do so when uncertain is a breach of your obligations.

Health Disclosure
Your safety — and your patients' safety — depends on honest disclosure

Why this matters: A non-disclosed pre-existing condition during patient handling can result in injury to you, risk to patient safety, and removal from placement. We have experienced this situation firsthand. Non-disclosure also creates complex liability and insurance consequences that can affect your career.

Enhance Physio is not your treating provider. If you sustain an injury during placement, you must report it immediately and seek care through your own healthcare provider. Students are not eligible for complimentary treatment from Enhance Physio staff.

Physical Health Disclosure Checklist

Please review the following. If any apply to you, you must notify your Supervisor before your placement commences. You do not need to share a diagnosis — simply indicate that you have a relevant condition that may affect the listed activities.

Mental Health & Wellbeing

Clinical placement can be emotionally demanding. Exposure to complex patient presentations, fatigue, and performance pressure are common stressors. We take student mental health seriously.

  • If you are experiencing a mental health condition that may affect your ability to safely and professionally participate in placement, you must notify your Supervisor or university coordinator before commencing.
  • You do not need to disclose a specific diagnosis — simply that you may need additional support or adjustments.
  • If your mental health status changes during placement, notify your Supervisor promptly.
  • Enhance Physio is not a mental health treatment provider. Students are encouraged to access their university's wellbeing services.

During-Placement Health Changes

If your physical or mental health status changes at any point during placement, you must:

  1. Notify your Supervisor on the same day
  2. Notify your university placement coordinator
  3. Not continue with tasks that present a risk to yourself or patients until cleared to do so

Work Health & Safety
Your obligations and rights in a safe clinical workplace

Work health and safety (WHS) legislation applies to you as a student on placement. You have both rights and responsibilities under this framework, regardless of your employment status.

Your WHS Rights

  • The right to a safe workplace free from hazards
  • The right to refuse unsafe work — you must inform your Supervisor and document the refusal
  • The right to be informed about risks relevant to your placement activities
  • The right to be trained before performing tasks with a safety component

Your WHS Responsibilities

  • Follow all safe work procedures and your Supervisor's reasonable safety instructions
  • Report all hazards, near-misses, and incidents — even if no injury occurred
  • Do not undertake tasks you have not been trained or authorised to perform
  • Use personal protective equipment (PPE) when required
  • Maintain appropriate manual handling techniques at all times

Manual Handling

Physiotherapy placements involve patient-handling activities including transfers, mobility assistance, and exercise support. You must:

  • Only assist with manual handling tasks under direct supervision
  • Never attempt to transfer or mobilise a patient alone unless explicitly authorised and trained to do so
  • Use equipment (hoists, gait belts, transfer boards) as directed — never improvise
  • Stop and seek assistance if you feel a task is beyond your physical capacity

Infection Control

  • Perform hand hygiene before and after every patient contact
  • Use standard precautions for all patients, regardless of known infection status
  • Do not attend placement if you are unwell with a communicable illness (flu, gastro, COVID-19, etc.)
  • Report any needlestick or sharps injuries immediately to your Supervisor

Emergency Procedures

On your first day, your Supervisor must show you:

  • The location of the nearest first aid kit and AED (defibrillator)
  • The emergency evacuation route and assembly point for your site
  • How to call for help in a clinical emergency (code procedure if applicable)
  • The location of the nearest emergency call button or staff alarm (if present)

In any emergency — call 000 first. Notify your Supervisor immediately after. Do not delay emergency services while attempting to contact clinic management.

Incident Reporting

All incidents — including near-misses — must be reported on the same shift. This includes:

  • Any injury sustained by you or a patient
  • Equipment failures
  • Falls or near-falls
  • Adverse patient reactions to treatment
  • Any event that could have caused harm but did not

Report to your Supervisor first, then follow the process outlined in the Reporting section of this handbook.

Professional Conduct
Boundaries, communication, and what professionalism looks like in practice

Professional Boundaries with Patients

Maintaining clear professional boundaries is one of the most important skills you will develop as a clinician. Boundaries protect patients, protect you, and protect the clinic.

✅ Appropriate behaviours include:
  • Addressing patients by their preferred name after asking
  • Using clear, professional language appropriate to the patient's level of understanding
  • Maintaining appropriate physical contact — always within your delegated scope and with consent
  • Being warm and empathetic while remaining professional
  • Discussing clinical matters only in appropriate clinical settings
❌ Boundary violations include:
  • Sharing personal details about yourself beyond what is necessary for rapport
  • Accepting gifts from patients (beyond minor, token items — discuss with Supervisor)
  • Continuing a therapeutic relationship outside the clinic setting
  • Engaging with patients on social media or via personal communication channels
  • Discussing other patients or staff with a patient
  • Making physical contact beyond what is clinically authorised

Professional Boundaries with Staff

You are a guest in our workplace. The relationships you build with staff are professional relationships. This includes:

Social Media & Personal Communication: You must not initiate or accept personal social media connections (Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, Facebook, etc.) with Enhance Physio staff members during your placement. Professional connections on LinkedIn are acceptable only after your placement has concluded.

Do not share personal contact details (personal phone number, personal email) with staff unless required by your university for placement administration. All placement-related communication should be through official channels.

Any inappropriate communication — whether initiated by you or a staff member — must be reported to the Clinical Director immediately.

Communication Standards

  • Be clear, respectful, and professional in all verbal and written communication
  • Do not use clinical jargon with patients without explanation
  • Never make clinical promises to patients (e.g. "you'll be better in two weeks")
  • Escalate any patient communication you are unsure how to handle to your Supervisor
  • Written communication (emails, documentation) must be reviewed by your Supervisor before sending or filing

Feedback & Performance

You will receive regular feedback during your placement. This is a professional process, not a personal one. You are expected to:

  • Receive feedback openly and non-defensively
  • Act on feedback promptly
  • Seek feedback proactively — do not wait to be told
  • Raise any concerns about your placement experience with your Supervisor or university coordinator

Privacy & Confidentiality
Patient information is never yours to share

Your confidentiality obligations do not end when your placement ends. Patient information you encounter during placement remains strictly confidential — indefinitely. This is a legal and ethical obligation.

What is Confidential Information?

Everything. This includes:

  • Patient names, dates of birth, contact details
  • Diagnoses, medical history, treatment plans
  • Funding schemes and financial information
  • Conversations between patients and clinicians
  • Patient outcomes and progress notes
  • Any information you observe, overhear, or access during placement

The Australian Privacy Act & Your Obligations

Enhance Physio operates in compliance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). As a student on placement, you are bound by the same obligations as our employees. Breach of patient privacy may result in:

  • Immediate removal from placement
  • Notification to your university
  • Referral to the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA)
  • Legal consequences under privacy legislation

Practical Rules

  • Never discuss patient details in public areas of the clinic (waiting room, corridors)
  • Never discuss patients outside the clinic — not with family, friends, or on social media
  • Do not access patient records beyond what is required for your directly supervised clinical tasks
  • If you use de-identified case studies for academic work, obtain explicit approval from your Supervisor first
  • Never take photos of patient records, treatment notes, or any patient-identifiable material
  • If you believe a privacy breach has occurred, report it immediately to your Supervisor

Electronic Systems & Records

Access to clinical software (e.g. Cliniko) is provided for supervised purposes only. You must:

  • Never share login credentials
  • Log out of clinical systems when not in use
  • Only access records of patients you are directly involved in caring for
  • Report any accidental access to records you should not have seen

Social Media & Communications
What you post, share, or send reflects on your profession

"Private" does not mean confidential. Anything posted online — regardless of your privacy settings — can be shared, screenshotted, and distributed. AHPRA has published explicit guidance on social media use for health practitioners, and the same standards apply to students.

Absolute Prohibitions

  • Never post photos, videos, or audio recordings taken at an Enhance Physio clinic without written authorisation
  • Never post information — even de-identified — that could allow a patient to be identified
  • Never post negative comments about Enhance Physio, its staff, or patients on any platform
  • Never tag Enhance Physio locations in personal posts without approval
  • Never connect with or follow patients on any personal social media platform

Staff Social Media Connections

As covered in the Professional Conduct section: do not initiate or accept personal social media connections with Enhance Physio staff during your placement. LinkedIn connections after placement has concluded are acceptable.

What You May Do

  • Follow Enhance Physio's official social media accounts
  • Share Enhance Physio's published content (e.g. clinic posts, articles) from official accounts
  • Mention your placement in general terms (e.g. "Completing my clinical placement at Enhance Physio") without any clinical details

Messaging & Personal Communication

  • Do not add patients or staff to personal messaging groups (WhatsApp, iMessage, etc.)
  • All placement-related communication must use your university or professional email
  • If a patient attempts to contact you through personal channels, inform your Supervisor immediately

AHPRA Reference: The Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA) has published social media guidance for health practitioners. As a student entering the health professions, you are encouraged to read this guidance and understand how it will apply to your career. Your conduct as a student sets the standard for your professional life.

Reporting & Incident Flowchart
Who to tell, when, and how

When something goes wrong — or even when it nearly goes wrong — the right response is always to report it. Prompt, honest reporting protects patients, protects you, and protects the clinic.

Key principle: All significant reporting — including incidents, complaints, and concerns — must include your Clinical Lead or Clinical Director. Do not attempt to resolve significant matters between yourself and one staff member in isolation.

General Reporting Flowchart

1
Ensure immediate safety
If anyone is in immediate danger — patient, student, or staff — ensure safety first. Call 000 if required. Do not delay emergency services to report to management.
2
Notify your Supervisor
Tell your on-site Supervisor as soon as the immediate situation is safe. Do this verbally first, then in writing. If your Supervisor is unavailable, go directly to the Clinical Lead.
3
Notify the Clinical Lead or Clinical Director
For any significant incident — injury, complaint, boundary concern, or policy breach — the Clinical Lead or Clinical Director must be informed on the same day. This is not optional.
4
Notify your University Placement Coordinator
For incidents that affect your placement, wellbeing, or academic standing, notify your university coordinator. Enhance Physio may also contact them directly for serious matters.
5
Complete written documentation
An incident report must be completed the same day. Your Supervisor will guide you through this process. Write factually and objectively — what happened, when, who was involved, what action was taken.

Real-World Scenarios
Practice thinking through situations before they happen

The following scenarios are based on real situations encountered during student placements. Read each one and consider what the correct course of action would be before expanding the answer.

🤕
Scenario 1: Pre-existing Injury & Patient Safety
Health Disclosure
▼
During a patient transfer, you feel a sharp pain in your lower back — an area you injured six months ago playing sport, which you did not disclose before placement. You complete the transfer, the patient is safe, but you are now in significant pain. You say nothing and continue the shift.
What went wrong: You had a pre-existing condition that should have been disclosed. By not disclosing it, you placed both yourself and the patient at risk during the transfer. Continuing to work while in pain compounds the risk.
What you should have done (before placement): Disclosed the condition to your Supervisor so modifications to your tasks could be arranged — without necessarily disclosing your specific diagnosis.
What you must do now: Stop the task. Inform your Supervisor immediately. Complete an incident report. Seek your own medical care. Do not return to manual handling until cleared. Your university coordinator must also be notified.
Important: Enhance Physio will not provide complimentary treatment. This is a matter of liability, patient safety, and non-disclosure — not a reflection of our care for you as a person.
📱
Scenario 2: Social Media & Professional Boundaries
Boundaries
▼
A staff member adds you on Instagram during your second week of placement. They follow you, and you follow back. Over the next few days they send you DMs commenting on your personal posts. You feel uncomfortable but don't want to cause issues at work.
What went wrong: A professional boundary has been crossed by the staff member. Accepting the follow created an ambiguous personal-professional dynamic that can escalate.
What you should do: You are not obligated to maintain personal social media connections with staff. Remove or restrict the connection. Then — importantly — report the situation to the Clinical Director directly and the same day. You will not be penalised for doing so.
What you should not do: Continue the interaction. Attempt to resolve it yourself without notifying the Clinical Director. Say nothing and hope it stops.
Remember: Feeling uncomfortable is a signal, not a problem. Reporting protects you, the staff member, and future students.
🗣️
Scenario 3: Patient Asks for Your Clinical Opinion
Scope of Practice
▼
While assisting in a group class, a patient asks you privately: "Do you think I need surgery? My physio hasn't mentioned it but I'm worried." You have some knowledge of this patient's condition and have an opinion. Your Supervisor is across the room.
What you must do: Do not share your opinion. Acknowledge their concern warmly, and let them know you are not in a position to give clinical advice — but that it is a great question for their physio. Tell them you will let the treating physiotherapist know they have this question.
Why: Providing clinical opinions is outside your scope as a student. Even if you are right, you may be wrong — and acting on incorrect student advice about surgery could cause serious harm. Your role is to support, not to diagnose or recommend.
After the session: Let your Supervisor know the patient had this question, so the treating clinician can follow it up appropriately.
🏊
Scenario 4: Hydrotherapy & Undisclosed Condition
Hydrotherapy
▼
You are placed at a site with a hydrotherapy pool. You have a skin condition (eczema) that is currently flaring. You feel awkward mentioning it and decide not to say anything, entering the pool during a group session.
What went wrong: Skin conditions relevant to pool participation must be disclosed prior to entry. An active flare may increase risk of infection for you and other pool users. Non-disclosure is a breach of your health declaration obligations.
What you should have done: Informed your Supervisor before the session that you have a skin condition and asked whether it was appropriate for you to enter the pool. This is a brief, clinical conversation — not an overshare.
The outcome of disclosure: Your Supervisor might advise you not to enter the pool for this session, or may confirm it is fine with appropriate management. Either way, you are protected and the patient population is protected.
😰
Scenario 5: Student Mental Health During Placement
Wellbeing
▼
You are three weeks into placement. You find yourself crying in the bathroom between patients. You are not sleeping. You feel overwhelmed and are struggling to concentrate. You don't want to seem weak or have your placement terminated, so you say nothing.
What you should do: Speak to someone — your Supervisor, your university placement coordinator, or your university's student wellbeing service. You do not need to have a diagnosis to ask for support.
Why speaking up matters: A student who is significantly distressed cannot safely provide patient care. Saying nothing is a risk to you and to patients. Seeking help early protects both.
What Enhance Physio will do: We will work with you and your university to find a solution — whether that is a temporary adjustment, a pause, or additional support. Our goal is not to penalise you for struggling. It is to keep everyone safe.
Resources: Contact your university's student support services. If you are in crisis, contact Lifeline on 13 11 14 or Beyond Blue on 1300 22 4636.
